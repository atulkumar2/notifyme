üëÅÔ∏è Eye Blink Reminders

What happens when you don't blink enough?

  • Digital Eye Strain (Computer Vision Syndrome): When focused on screens, people blink 66% less. This causes dry, tired, and irritated eyes.
  • Reduced Tear Production: Blinking spreads tears across your eyes. Fewer blinks mean less moisture, leading to discomfort and vision problems.
  • Eye Fatigue: Continuous focus on a single distance strains the eye muscles, causing headaches and fatigue.
  • Long-term Damage: Chronic dry eyes can lead to corneal abrasion and vision quality degradation.

How blinking helps: Regular blinking refreshes your eyes with natural tears, reduces strain, and gives your eye muscles a brief rest. Following the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds) is highly recommended by optometrists.

üö∂ Walking Reminders

What happens when you sit too long?

  • Poor Blood Circulation: Prolonged sitting restricts blood flow, increasing risk of blood clots, varicose veins, and cardiovascular issues.
  • Muscle Weakness: Sedentary behavior weakens muscles, reduces metabolism, and can lead to weight gain and muscle atrophy.
  • Postural Problems: Sitting for extended periods causes poor posture, leading to back pain, neck strain, and spinal issues.
  • Mental Health Impact: Lack of movement decreases endorphins and increases stress and anxiety. Movement improves mood and mental clarity.
  • Increased Health Risks: Studies show that sitting more than 8 hours daily significantly increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, and early mortality.

How walking helps: Even brief walking breaks (5-10 minutes) improve circulation, strengthen muscles, improve posture, boost mood, and increase productivity. Short movement breaks every hour can reduce health risks by up to 30%.

üíß Water Hydration Reminders

What happens when you're dehydrated?

  • Brain Function Decline: Even mild dehydration (2% loss) impairs concentration, memory, and decision-making abilities. Your brain is 75% water!
  • Energy Loss: Dehydration reduces oxygen delivery to cells, causing fatigue, weakness, and reduced physical performance.
  • Headaches: One of the most common causes of workplace headaches is insufficient water intake.
  • Physical Performance: Dehydration affects muscle function, coordination, and endurance during any physical activity.
  • Kidney and Digestive Issues: Inadequate water intake can lead to kidney stones, constipation, and urinary tract infections.
  • Temperature Regulation: Your body uses water to regulate temperature. Dehydration can lead to overheating and heat-related illness.

How hydration helps: Regular water intake maintains optimal body function, improves cognitive performance, boosts energy levels, aids digestion, and supports overall health. The recommended intake is 8-10 glasses daily, but needs vary based on activity and climate.
