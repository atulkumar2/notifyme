â“ Why These Reminders Matter

ğŸ‘ï¸ Blink Reminders

The Problem: When staring at screens, people blink 66% less, causing digital eye strain, dry eyes, and fatigue.

  • Reduced tear production leads to eye discomfort
  • Eye muscles become fatigued from continuous focus
  • Can cause long-term vision problems

The Solution: Regular blinking refreshes eyes with natural tears, reduces strain, and prevents vision degradation. Following the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds) is recommended by optometrists.

ğŸš¶ Walking Reminders

The Problem: Prolonged sitting restricts blood flow, weakens muscles, and increases health risks.

  • Poor blood circulation increases heart disease and clot risks
  • Muscle weakness and metabolic slowdown
  • Postural problems leading to back and neck pain
  • Mental health impact: reduced mood and increased stress

The Solution: Even brief 5-minute walking breaks improve circulation, strengthen muscles, improve posture, boost mood, and increase productivity. Short movement breaks can reduce health risks by up to 30%.

ğŸ’§ Hydration Reminders

The Problem: Even mild dehydration (2% fluid loss) significantly impairs cognitive function.

  • Reduced concentration and memory (your brain is 75% water)
  • Fatigue and reduced physical performance
  • Common cause of workplace headaches
  • Affects kidney function and digestion

The Solution: Regular water intake maintains optimal brain function, improves energy levels, prevents headaches, and supports overall health. The recommended intake is 8-10 glasses daily.

ğŸ§˜ Pranayama Reminders

The Problem: Long periods of focused work can raise stress and shallow breathing patterns.

  • Shallow breathing reduces oxygen intake
  • Stress builds without intentional pauses
  • Reduced focus and mental fatigue over time

The Solution: Short pranayama breaks (2-3 minutes) help reset the nervous system, improve calm focus, and reduce tension.
